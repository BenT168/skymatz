Full Description

Morning Stroll at West Lake (Duanqiao to Bai Causeway): Start your day with a tranquil walk along the Bai Causeway, embraced by shimmering waters and gentle morning light. Listen to the legend of the White Snake, gaze toward the distant islands of Three Pools Mirroring the Moon, and feel how this UNESCO World Heritage Site blends nature with ancient poetry.

Southern Song Imperial Street & Hefang Street: Wander through the old commercial heart of Hangzhou. This lively pedestrian street still echoes with the spirit of the Southern Song Dynasty—lined with time-honored shops, traditional pharmacies, teahouses, and handicraft stores. A glimpse into the city’s thousand-year-old trading culture.

China National Silk Museum: Visit the largest silk museum in the world and discover how Hangzhou became known as the “Silk Capital.” Exhibitions span from ancient looms and imperial robes to contemporary fashion, offering a vivid journey through Chinese textile arts, aesthetics, and cultural symbolism.

Mantoushan Creative Community: In this quiet, artsy neighborhood, abandoned factories and old homes have been transformed by young creatives into cafés, studios, and galleries. Tucked away from the crowds, Mantoushan offers a refreshing encounter with Hangzhou’s modern lifestyle shaped by tradition and imagination.

Cloisonné Enamel Keychain Workshop: Dive into a hands-on cultural experience by creating your own cloisonné enamel keychain. Guided by a professional artisan, you'll design, wire, and color your piece using a centuries-old metalworking technique. A beautiful memory—and a unique souvenir—you make yourself.

Xiaohezhijie Historic Street: A Canal-Side Farewell: End the day strolling down one of the best-preserved old streets by the Grand Canal. With stone pavements, arching bridges, artisan boutiques, and local teahouses, Xiaohezhijie blends history and daily life into one charming riverside escape—perfect for a slow, soulful goodbye to Hangzhou.
