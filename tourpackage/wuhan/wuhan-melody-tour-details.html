Highlights

  • Visit a rare Buddhist temple with Gothic domes hidden in central Wuhan
  • Stroll along the Yangtze River with skyline views and city breeze

Full Description

Gude Temple: Begin your day in a hidden architectural gem—this Buddhist temple features rare domes and pillars resembling European cathedrals. Built in the late Qing dynasty, it offers a serene spiritual start.

Hankou Riverside Promenade: Take a peaceful walk along the wide riverside boulevard. Watch boats drift by, see the skyline unfold, and feel the pulse of a river that shaped a city.

Jiqing Folk Street: Step into a street full of local color—opera murals, street food, folk musicians, and traditional crafts. This is real Wuhan life, served with a side of nostalgia.

Hubei Provincial Museum: Home to the world-famous Zenghouyi bronze bells and ancient swords, this museum brings to life the grandeur and elegance of central China's early civilizations.

Han Street Walk & Han Show Theater Exterior: Take a relaxed stroll through the European-style boulevard. Admire the red lantern-shaped theater designed by Cirque du Soleil’s creators—a symbol of modern Wuhan.

Tanhualin Historic Quarter & Hands-on Craft Session: Explore a reborn old street full of coffee shops, studios, graffiti, and bookstores. This is where young Wuhan dreams in color, tucked inside centuries-old bricks. Get creative in a local artisan’s studio. Choose to paint a lacquer fan or stitch a fragrant sachet with traditional Chinese herbs—a unique souvenir you make yourself.

Yellow Crane Tower View & Qingchuan Pavilion: View the iconic tower from across the river, then walk part of the bridge for breathtaking city and river views. End the day at the poetic Qingchuan Pavilion facing the Yangtze. With the river glowing gold in sunset light, it’s the perfect scene to wrap up your Wuhan adventure.
