Full Description

Zhonggu Cable Car Ride: Xiamen’s most scenic viewpoints. As you glide through lush mountain greenery, enjoy panoramic views of the city’s red rooftops, distant coastline, and the vast South China Sea. It's a tranquil and photogenic start to your cultural journey.

Nanputuo Temple: A serene Buddhist sanctuary dating back to the Tang Dynasty. Stroll past fragrant incense burners, stone pagodas, and lotus ponds. The temple’s architecture reflects centuries of religious devotion, while its peaceful hilltop location offers calmness and reflection amid the city bustle.

Shapowei Art Zone: A former fishing harbor transformed into Xiamen’s most creative district. Here, old boatyards and warehouses now house independent galleries, street murals, cafés, and vintage shops. Walk the lanes and soak in the fusion of marine heritage and youthful artsy energy that defines modern Xiamen.

Lacquer Fan Workshop: stop at a local craft studio to join a hands-on lacquer fan workshop. Guided by a local artist, you’ll learn how to create your own folding fan using traditional lacquer techniques such as “water ripple” or natural crackle. This experience combines artistry with heritage—and you get to take home a beautiful souvenir.

Mountain-to-Sea Trail (Shanhai Path): Walk off lunch with a scenic stroll along a section of the Mountain-to-Sea Trail. This 50-minute elevated walk blends city skyline views with forested paths and glimpses of the sea. The trail is easy to navigate and perfect for those wanting fresh air and quiet moments.

Xiamen 8th Market (Bashi): The most authentic and lively traditional market in the city. Bustling with vendors selling fresh seafood, dry goods, flowers, and local snacks, this spot offers a true slice of daily Xiamen life. It’s noisy, colorful, and a dream for street photography and food lovers.

Zhongshan Road: Wrap up the day at Zhongshan Road, Xiamen’s iconic shopping and night market strip. Lined with colonial-style arcade buildings and glowing neon signs, the street comes alive after sunset. Enjoy free time to explore street food stalls, grab some souvenirs, or simply enjoy the city lights and crowd buzz before heading back.
